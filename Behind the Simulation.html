Behind the Simulation

This Web-based simulation was developed with a simple objective to make BOF oxygen jet penetration depth easier to understand, visualize, and apply.This project represents my effort to bridge the gap between by transforming theoritical engineering knowledge into an intutive, interactive, and dynamic tool. This model has continuously evolved through technical discussions, industrial observations, and constant guidance and encouragement of my mentor.

Prepared By Rangan Banerjee
Mentor Dr. Bitan Sarkar

In Basic Oxygen Furnace (BOF) steelmaking, the interaction between the supersonic oxygen jet and the molten steel bath is the primary driver of metallurgical efficiency and reaction kinetics.

The depth to which the oxygen jet penetrates the liquid bath directly dictates the rate of decarburization, slag formation, and overall bath mixing.

If the penetration is too shallow, the jet fails to mix the bath effectively, leading to excessive iron oxidation (high FeO in the slag) and severe slopping. Conversely, if the penetration is too deep, the jet violently scours the furnace floor, resulting in accelerated refractory wear and a drastically reduced campaign life for the converter vessel.

Engineering Objective
The primary objective of this BOF Jet Penetration Depth Simulation is to provide process engineers and metallurgists with a dynamic, real-time visualization tool that mathematically estimates the physical impact cavity of the oxygen jet. By simulating how variations in lance height, oxygen flow rate, and nozzle geometry interact with scale-dependent BOF vessels, the tool allows users to safely optimize blowing profiles, maximize oxygen efficiency, and protect refractory lining.

The simulator calculates the physical jet cavity utilizing a empirical framework based on the established Masazumi and Segawa models.

I. Oxygen Lance Design & Process Parameters

The behavior of the oxygen jet is governed by static geometric design parameters made during lance manufacturing and dynamic process adjustments made by the operator.

Oxygen Flow Rate VO₂
This is the driving force of oxygen jet. More oxygen delivered per unit time increases the kinetic energy of the supersonic jet, resulting in deeper penetration into the liquid bath.
Key Observation
Increasing Oxygen Flow Rate (VO₂) generally increases jet penetration depth.
Lance Height hL
The most important operating parameter, lance height determines the distance between the nozzle exit and the liquid bath surface. This directly affects the jet's momentum transfer and the degree of ambient gas entrainment and velocity decay.
Key Observation
Higher lance allows entrainment of ambient gas, resulting in a wider, shallower cavity.
Number of Nozzles n
The total oxygen flow is divided among individual nozzles. This controls how the jet momentum is distributed across the bath surface.
Key Observation
More nozzles reduce the momentum per nozzle, resulting in a shallower penetration cavity.
Nozzle Angle θ
The inclination angle of the nozzles off the vertical axis determines the radial spread of the oxygen jets.
Key Observation
A larger angle pushes jets toward the vessel walls, slightly reducing downward vertical momentum.
Nozzle Diameter d
The throat diameter of the copper nozzle dictates gas expansion and absolute exit velocity.
Key Observation
Wider exit diameter diffuses the jet momentum, flattening the impact cavity.

II. BOF Vessel Geometry & Heat Size Parameters

The vessel parameters dictate the physical environment and liquid bath depth that the jet interacts with.

Total Refractory Lining = Safety Lining (15%) + Working Lining (85%)

The Refractory Lining determines the effective internal BOF cavity. This establishes the Static Bath Height (L₀), which is the critical denominator used to calculate the Penetration Index (PI).

Important Geometric Relation
Modifying the Heat Size changes the vessel geometry, wall thickness, and static liquid bath depth, which directly affects the Penetration Index (PI) and the region of the blowing zone. The vessel heat size is interpolated from historical BOF vessel design data used in the industry.

While the current mathematical model provides highly accurate estimations of pure jet-to-metal momentum transfer, future updates are planned to incorporate broader multi-phase metallurgical phenomena.

Planned physics and operational upgrades include:

  • Dynamic lance movement tracking.
  • Slag layer and emulsion height modelling.
  • Bottom gas stirring and upward buoyancy integration.
  • Simplified heat transfer and reaction kinetics mapping.
  • Predictive refractory wear profiling.

Digital Twin & RAG Integration

The ultimate vision for this tool is to evolve it into a fully integrated Digital Twin with Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) capabilities. Future versions will integrate an underlying engineering knowledge base built from BOF literature, textbooks, operating manuals, research papers, and industrial documentation.

Rather than simply generating responses, the integrated assistant would retrieve validated engineering knowledge before providing recommendations. Potential capabilities include:

  • Explaining complex BOF process concepts.
  • Interpreting anomalous simulation outputs.
  • Suggesting optimum operating parameters.
  • Oxygen requirement and blowing time estimation.
  • Heat balance calculations.
  • Lime and flux estimation based on input chemistry.
  • Slag chemistry guidance.
  • Dynamic, real-time operating recommendations.
  • Engineering troubleshooting for process deviations.
  • Reference-supported answers linked directly to manuals.
Platform Evolution
This integration would transform the simulator from a standalone penetration depth calculator into an intelligent, AI-assisted engineering decision-support platform.
